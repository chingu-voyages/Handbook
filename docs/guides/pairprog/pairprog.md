# Pair Challenge Guide

![Team creating project backlog](./assets/PairProgramming_coders.jpeg)

## Overview

Pair programming is a proven technique that improves code quality, helps developers share knowledge, and builds collaboration skills. At Chingu, we extend this practice to all roles — not just Developers. Scrum Masters and Product Owners benefit just as much from solving challenges together as Developers do.

Through Chingu Pair Challenges you will:

- Meet new people and solve a challenge together
- Share what you know and take your skills to the next level
- Learn new technologies and techniques from your peers

Every week we match people who want to participate in Pair Challenges to help them level up.

To learn more about Pair Programming, check out this [article](https://stackify.com/pair-programming-advantages/).

## Prerequisites

Pair Challenge sessions are started by you. For now, join the #🟢Lobby channel to begin the process. In the future you'll be able to schedule sessions ahead of time.

## Your Responsibilities

Your main responsibilities are to learn how Pair Challenges work, help your partner achieve the same goal, and hopefully make a new friend along the way.

This means you need to be responsive to your partner and show up for your scheduled session(s).

## Steps to Follow

1. Sign up for a Pair Challenge session by joining the #🟢Lobby channel on Discord. You cannot talk in the lobby — just join it. More info is also available in the #🍐pair-challenge channel.
2. Our bot, Parity, will send you a DM. Follow the steps it gives you. Make sure your privacy settings allow DMs from server members.
3. Choose your role after signing up. Only Developer is available right now — Agile Leader is coming soon.
4. Choose a difficulty level based on what you're comfortable with. Challenge difficulty is shown on each challenge.
5. Wait for someone with the same role and difficulty to sign up, or ask around in the server to find a partner.
6. Once paired, the bot will create a private space for the two of you where you'll select a challenge.
   - Discuss carefully which challenge you want to take before selecting one.
   - Leaving the #🟢Lobby channel **before** you've been matched will remove you from matchmaking.

> We suggest using Discord's built-in conferencing and screen sharing, but you can use any collaboration tool you prefer.
> For technical questions, post in the [`#coding-help`](https://discord.com/channels/330284646283608064/1047615334703714394) channel. For problems with the matching process or the challenge itself, [open a ticket](https://discord.com/channels/330284646283608064/1193342042080817323).

## Tips

1. Create a voice channel by joining the [Voice Hub Channel](https://discord.com/channels/330284646283608064/1130565532953882675). Use it to talk with your partner and share your screen.
2. You'll really want to solve the problem — but remember that the most important goal is to practice communication, collaboration, and teamwork. Those are the skills employers look for, and the ones that will set you apart from other job candidates.
